About Kwabena Afriyie Poku

Biography

Kwabena Afriyie Poku (b. 1968) is a multidisciplinary artist and educator based in Kumasi, Ghana. As a faculty member at the prestigious Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology, Poku bridges the worlds of academia and artistic practice, bringing a unique perspective to his work.Kwabena Afriyie Poku (b. 1968) is a multidisciplinary artist and educator based in Kumasi, Ghana. As a faculty member at the prestigious Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology, Poku bridges the worlds of academia and artistic practice, bringing a unique perspective to his work.

Artist Statement

Artist Statement: The human body, a boundless source of expression, growth, and survival, has been an enduring wellspring of innovation. Its influence extends beyond the realm of the physical, permeating our creative endeavors throughout history. Within the intricate choreography of our movements, we discover the origins of our narratives and experiences, foundational elements that define us as profoundly as our bones, vessels, and organs.In our exploration of the world, we have uncovered a profound means of preserving and revisiting our stories—through drawing. This practice not only allows us to transcend cultural boundaries but also serves as a bridge between seemingly disparate disciplines. Drawing rituals, enriched by cultural values and customs, facilitate social interactions by weaving connections among individuals, objects, and spaces.Within the realm of my artistic expression, I cordially invite you to experience "Traversing Kata: Body Movements as Drawing Paths." In this captivating journey, I delve into the world of kata paths, where the ancient art of movement intertwines seamlessly with the timeless craft of drawing. These paths, embodied by the grace of Nijushiho, serve as both my canvas and inspiration, rekindling dialogues between performers, support staff, and the audience. As I meticulously trace the repetitive, intricate movements of these performances, I find myself echoing experiences and interactions of bygone eras. Through this intricate dance of movement and artistry, I breathe vitality into memories, and experiences, drawing them onto the very surface of time through the mesmerizing cadence of kata Kwabena Afriyie Poku (b. 1968) is an artist and educator in KNUST's Department of Painting and Sculpture, with program expertise in human anatomical drawing, digital imaging, figure and general drawing, experimental painting, and graphic communication. He explores human body movement via drawing, karate, multimedia/digital art, and performances. Kwabena has participated in several national and international exhibitions, including Odic Organisms in 2023, Digital Painting Exhibitions in 2022, Project Art Exhibition for Advancing Creative Industries for Development in Ghana (ACIG) in 2020, a coupled performance and conference presentation titled Ancient Rivers and Current Trends at the 3rd African Martial Arts Conference, hosted by UNESCO (Accra) in 2019, and SPECTACLE SPECTACULAR at blaxTARLINES Contemporary Art Center, Kumasi in 2018. He has also illustrated numerous publications, including 2014's award-winning Once Upon a Time in Ghana. Kwabena Poku coordinates and participates in the yearly OFKOB Artist Residence.
